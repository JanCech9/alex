What I actually do

I have a master's degree in psychology and I support people in difficulties, both everyday and those that emerge out of nowhere after a long time. My history of helping work has taken on many different forms: from running workshops for students, through supporting non-governmental organizations in anti-discrimination education, to office work. The most important aspects of working with people for me are openness and a subjective approach to the client. I want the space during our meetings to be safe and allow for free attention to the issues you report. It is up to you what we will work on and in what form, because we meet for a reason, right?

About me

I provide psychological consultations and support the development of adults. I work with clients individually, providing psychoeducation and counseling on topics including communication, relationships, identity issues, and sexuality. The office is a safe space for human diversity, my job is not to judge, but to support and help to the extent that the person reporting is ready. In addition to my office practice, I also organize sex education workshops for adults."
